Engineering Physics:

Kinematics:
Kinematics is the branch of physics that studies the motion of objects without considering the forces causing the motion.

Laws of Motion:
Newton's laws of motion explain the relationship between the motion of an object and the forces acting on it.

Work Energy and Power:
Work is done when a force moves an object. Energy is the ability to do work. Power is the rate of doing work.

Oscillations:
Oscillation is the repetitive motion of an object about its equilibrium position.

Waves:
A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another without transferring matter.

Optics:
Optics is the branch of physics that studies light and its behavior.


Engineering Mathematics:

Matrices:
A matrix is a rectangular array of numbers arranged in rows and columns.

Differentiation:
Differentiation is the process of finding the rate of change of a function.

Integration:
Integration is the process of finding the area under a curve.

Differential Equations:
A differential equation is an equation that involves derivatives.


Programming in C:

Variables:
Variables are used to store data values.

Loops:
Loops are used to repeat a block of code multiple times.

Functions:
Functions are reusable blocks of code that perform specific tasks.

Arrays:
An array is a collection of elements stored at contiguous memory locations.

Pointers:
Pointers are variables that store memory addresses.

Structures:
Structures are used to store multiple data types in a single variable.
